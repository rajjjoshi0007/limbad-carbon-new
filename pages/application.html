Water Treatment

Activated carbon is widely used in water treatment processes to remove impurities, organic compounds, chlorine, odor, and taste. It helps in improving the quality and safety of drinking water, as well as treating industrial wastewater and purifying process water in various industries.

Benefits

  • Effective and Versatile
  • Improves Taste and Odor
  • Reduces Health Risks

Applications

  • Drinking water purification
  • Municipal waste water treatment
  • Industrial waste water treatment
  • Aquarium filters
  • Swimming pool water treatment
  • Waste water treatment
  • Removing Odors from wastewater
  • Desalination plant

Air & Gas Purification

The air in closed spaces like inside cars and rooms contains volatile organic compounds resulting from building materials, components, and organic compounds created from our bodies, cigarette smoke, and so on. Removing pollution using activated carbon and filters contributes to the improvement and sustainability of living environments.

Benefits

  • Highly Effective
  • Customizable
  • Reusable
  • Safe and Eco-friendly

Applications

  • Landfill gas treatment
  • Natural Gases
  • Flue Gas treatment
  • Hydrogen Sulfide removal
  • Acid gases treatment
  • Gas masks
  • Solvent recovery
  • Air Purifiers
  • Refrigerators and Air Conditioners
  • HVAC Systems
  • Gas Masks and Respirators

Cabin Air Filter

Double Defense cabin air filters combine two filtration mechanisms: a mechanical filter and an activated carbon layer. The mechanical filter captures larger particles like dust, pollen, and allergens, preventing them from entering your car's interior.

Benefits

  • Improved Air Quality
  • Reduced Allergic Reactions
  • Enhanced Comfort

Oil & Gas Treatment

Crude oil and natural gas can contain impurities like sulfur compounds, mercury, and certain organic molecules. These can be problematic for various reasons such as corrosion, toxicity, and product quality. The adsorption performance of activated carbon is effective in deodorizing industrial chemicals, refining cleaning liquid, and treating plating wastewater for reuse.

Benefits

  • Improved Product Quality
  • Enhanced Safety
  • Environmental Protection
  • Process Efficiency

Applications

  • Hydrocarbon Sweetening
  • Mercury Removal
  • Liquid Hydrocarbon Purification
  • Amine/Glycol Purification
  • Vapor Recovery

Food & Beverages

Activated carbon is employed in the food and beverage industry for purification, decolorization, and deodorization purposes. It helps remove impurities, contaminants, and unwanted odors from food and beverage ingredients, such as sugar, edible oils, and alcoholic beverages.

Benefits

  • Improved Product Quality
  • Enhanced Safety
  • Increased Efficiency

Applications

  • Sugar and Sweetener Purification
  • Juice Clarification
  • Wine and Beer Treatment
  • Edible Oil Processing
  • Decaffeination

Pharma & Medical

The adsorption performance of "Charcoal" was used as remedies to treat poisonings since ancient Egypt. Since then, activated carbon has been used not only as medicine itself but also for the production process of pharmaceutical products.

Benefits

  • Improved Drug Safety and Efficacy
  • Enhanced Patient Care
  • Efficiency in Dialysis

Applications

  • Internal Applications:
  • Poison Antidote
  • Gastrointestinal Relief
  • External Applications:
  • Wound Care
  • Kidney Dialysis
  • Manufacturing Processes:
  • Drug Purification
  • Process Water Purification

Cosmetics & Personal Care

Activated carbon, also known as activated charcoal, has become a popular ingredient in cosmetics and personal care products in recent years. This is due to its unique ability to adsorb, or trap, a variety of substances on its highly porous surface.

Benefits

  • Detoxification
  • Oil control
  • Exfoliation

Applications

  • Facial cleansers
  • Face masks
  • Scrubs
  • Soaps
  • Toothpaste

Gold Mining

Coconut Shell Activated Carbon is a popular choice due to its high hardness and resistance to abrasion in the gold recovery process.

Benefits

  • Efficient gold recovery from low-grade ores
  • Allows for continuous gold extraction
  • Can be regenerated for multiple uses

Process Steps

  • Crushing and Grinding
  • Leaching with Cyanide Solution
  • Gold Adsorption onto Activated Carbon
  • Desorption and Gold Recovery
